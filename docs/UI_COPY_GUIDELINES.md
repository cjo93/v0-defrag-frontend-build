# UI COPY GUIDELINES

The DEFRAG user interface must prioritize clarity, calm, and a sense of grounded understanding. All user-facing copy should be accessible, respectful, and free from hype or urgency.

## UX Writing Standards

To ensure DEFRAG remains accessible and supportive:

- **6th–8th grade reading level**: Keep vocabulary simple and sentence structures straightforward.
- **One idea per sentence**: Avoid complex, multi-clause sentences that require re-reading to understand.
- **No stacked clauses**: Break long sentences into shorter, clearer statements.
- **No marketing hype**: Do not use "game-changing," "revolutionary," or other exaggerated terms.
- **No urgency triggers**: Never pressure the user to "act fast," "don't miss out," or "do this now."
- **No streak mechanics language**: Avoid gamifying the experience with "streaks," "levels," or "points."
- **No gamification language**: Do not use words that imply winning, losing, or competing.

## Microcopy Guidelines

When writing short text elements like buttons, labels, and tooltips, keep it brief and action-oriented without being aggressive or overly technical.

- **Buttons**: Limit button text to 1–3 words.
- **Avoid “Submit”**: Use softer, more descriptive alternatives.
- **Avoid “Execute”**: Do not use mechanical or command-line language.
- **Avoid “Run”**: Similarly, avoid words that imply running a program or system process.

**Prefer:**

- “Continue”
- “Start”
- “Save”
- “Reflect”
